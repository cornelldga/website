Who Are We?

DGA is Cornell’s premier game development organization. We are a team of passionate and hardworking students interested in game development. Our purpose is to improve the skills of ourselves and others through rigorous and dynamic activities related to our passion. The executive board of DGA is a group of six students whose aim is to push the club to new heights this year by revolutionizing the ways students can engage with the club.

The game development industry is exploding, and Cornell’s game development club is following suit. DGA is breaking boundaries this year. Since last May, we have gone from a club that hosts weekly meetings with little outreach and interest, to a pillar of the Cornell art and technology communities alike. We have hosted many successful development campaigns and challenges this year, such as our winter and spring game jams, and our summer development project.

What We Do

Our mission is to empower students who want to engage in game development, no matter their skill level while spreading the love of games on and off campus.

  • 01

    DGA's Project Team

    A team composed of programmers, artists, and sound engineers that focuses on crafting a fully-fledged original game from the ground up within one academic year.

  • 02

    Workshops and Hands-on Experiences

    Engaging and interactive workshops providing hands-on experiences with industry-standard techniques and technologies.

  • 03

    Summer Game Development Project

    Students collaborate on making a game from scratch, developing their hard skills and ability to communicate and collaborate in a team.

  • 04

    Game Jams

    Development challenges hosted throughout the academic year where students of any experience level come together to develop their own games in a short amount of time.
